Guide to Necromancy

Necromancy as a label varies a lot in what it means. It's fascinating to see the origin of the word in Ancient Greece, referring to the specific practice of foretelling the future using the dead.

Then by the early medieval period it had broadened to refer to any form of magic that was disincentivized. I say disincentivized, because mysticism and magical rituals were allowed. The early medieval Catholic Church, officially, did not acknowledge pagan magic as having power. If something that seems like magic occurs it is only able to occur by the grace of God.
